ABOUT THE PROJECT

This project involved preparing a large dataset of over 50,000 restaurant reviews that initially contained many challenges such as missing values, inconsistent data types, duplicate records, and multi-valued fields. Through an extensive data cleaning and preprocessing phase, these issues were addressed to ensure data accuracy and consistency. The cleaned dataset was then uploaded into a MySQL database to provide a robust and organized foundation for efficient data management to enable reliable, data-driven insights to support business decision-making by the restaurant, ultimately improving understanding of customer review.

KEY ACTIVITIES PERFORMED

  • Established clear project objectives and success metrics to ensure data was clean and ready for meaningful analysis.
  • Performed a thorough review of raw datasets to assess structure, completeness, and consistency, identifying key data quality issues.
  • Standardized and normalized data by cleaning column names, correcting formats, and enforcing consistent data types for reliable processing.
  • Handled missing values with targeted imputation techniques where possible, and carefully removed records that needed to be removed to maintain data integrity.
  • Removed duplicate entries and ensured entity consistency through rule-based matching and deduplication methods.
  • Identified outliers through statistical analysis combined with domain expertise, and treated them appropriately to improve analysis accuracy.
  • Engineered new features and aggregated metrics to enhance analytical depth and data usability
  • Transformed and normalized multi-valued fields like Dish_liked, Rest_type, and Cuisines into relational tables using pandas, with keys to maintain referential integrity
  • Conducted rigorous validation of the clean dataset through summary statistics and spot checks, I also attached a documentation for easy understanding and reproducibility.
